The Kingdom of Elenoir is the elven kingdom deep in the Forest of Elshire of Dicathen. Located on the northen border of the continent, the kingdom is primarily composed of elves.

The Kingdom of Elenoir is established deep within the Forest of Elshire of Dicathen. It is located on the northern border of the continent where a dense array of branches that fan out from the trees cover most of the city in a canopy of shade. The capital of the Kingdom of Elenoir is the City of Zestier, where the royal family resides. The capital is filled with numerous floating orbs of light surrounding the city, creating a warm, luminescent glow. Buildings are made from jade-like material intertwined by large, tall trees, and homes are built on unnaturally thick branches extending out of massive trunks. The elven city is covered in a lush field of soft moss, with only narrow sidewalks and the main road paved with smooth stone.

Within the City of Zestier, a large enormous tree made of white stone inhabiting the home of the royal family. The interior of the castle had two curved staircases that created a circle, with a grand chandelier made from the same orbs of light that surrounded the city, floating in the center.

The Kingdom of Elenoir is a monarchy, which was ruled by Alduin Eralith prior to his death.

Elven Royalty


Elven royalty is dressed in fine clothing with golden accessories across their attire, signifying their wealth. Both the king and queen wear golden leaflet crowns on top of their heads.

Elven Warriors


Elven warriors guard the gates to the Kingdom of Elenoir and are dressed in coordinated black suits with green trimmings and a golden shoulder guard on their left shoulder. They carry a golden rapier strapped to their waist along with a black sash hung across their right shoulder. The elven warriors wear black fingerless gloves along with thick black armor hidden beneath their black buttoned up cloak.

The Kingdom of Sapin is the largest and most populated region of Dicathen. Located on the eastern border of the continent (western as seen in illustrations), the kingdom is primarily composed of humans, but there are also numerous merchants from the Dwarven race, trading many commodities. Moreover, Sapin has a symbiotic relationship with the Kingdom of Darv when it comes to resources.

Ashber Town


This town of Ashber was more of a glorified outpost, seeing that there were no roads or buildings. People walked on the main dirt trail, where there were tents on both sides with various merchants and salesmen selling all sorts of things—from common, everyday necessities to things Arthur couldn’t help but raise a brow at, like weapons, armor, and rocks... shining rocks!

Valden City
While operating in the city of Valden, Reynolds Leywin fell in love with an Adventurer Guild hall exam proctor named Alice Leywin, who he later married.

Xyrus City


Xyrus City is a major city in the Kingdom of Sapin situated on a floating island above the center of Dicathen. Arthur read in a book about the creation story of Xyrus City. According to the book, this floating piece of land was built by an ancient organization of conjurers for the sole purpose of housing the most prestigious Mage Academy. A city was later built around the academy and both the City and the academy were named after the leader of the organization - Xyrus. Nevertheless, the truth is that Xyrus City was created by the Djinn, which was mentioned later in the story.

Grand Mountains
Between the Forest of Elshire and the Kingdom of Sapin lies the Grand Mountain range which spans across roughly 90% of the continent, separating the North and East from the West and South. This mountain range is the place where Arthur Leywin and his family were attacked by a group of bandits.

Dragonspine Inn
The Dragonspine Inn is where Arthur and Jasmine decided to go pick up some money. Arthur ended up getting challenged by a B-Rank Adventurer and Arthur cuts his fingers off.



The Kingdom of Sapin is a monarchy, which is ruled by the Glayder Family.

Human Royalty


Human Royalty is dressed in fine clothing with golden accessories across their attire, signifying their wealth. Both the king and queen wear crowns on top of their heads.

Human Warriors


The Royal Knight Guards were Knight armour and seem to be Augmenters, except for Sebastian who was not only a Conjurer, but also dressed differently from the rest.

Hierarchy
This kingdom is a place of discrimination and classification. The hierarchical roots imbedded into this land would never truly disappear. Normal humans were considered second-rate people, while even amongst mages, augmenters were discriminated against by conjurers. It went further than that where, unless one was a deviant or a dual element specialist, some elements were considered "higher class" than others.

Slavery is still a thing in the Kingdom of Sapin unlike Arthur’s past world where slavery was abolished hundreds of years ago.

There also exists a division between nobles and commoners in this country.
